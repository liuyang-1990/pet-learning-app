# Separate Word Bank From Weak Words

The vocabulary system will separate the broad PET Word Bank from learner-specific Weak Words.

Daily Sessions introduce exactly five Daily New Words from the current Word Theme. The Guardian can choose the current theme, while the system may rotate themes weekly by default. Daily New Words are practised through understanding, British English shadowing, and simple English output.

Weak Words are not imported from the PET Word Bank automatically. A word becomes a Weak Word only when the learner shows a real problem: unclear pronunciation, recall failure, incorrect meaning, incorrect usage, or spelling/recognition error. Daily Sessions can include up to five Daily Weak Words in addition to the five Daily New Words.

Seen Words are Daily New Words that the learner handled successfully. They should be lightly resurfaced in prompts, examples, or small activities, but they do not join the main daily review queue unless the learner later struggles with them.

Weak Words carry a Weak Word Reason and should be practised according to that reason. A Weak Word is considered mastered after roughly two correct outcomes in the last three reviews, with clear pronunciation required for pronunciation issues. Correct natural use during Speaking Practice can accelerate mastery.

This avoids turning the app into a generic flashcard app, keeps PET speaking output central, and prevents the imported Cambridge vocabulary list from being mislabelled as learner weakness.
